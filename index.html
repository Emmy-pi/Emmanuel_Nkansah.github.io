    TELECOM CUSTOMER CHURN EDA AND PREDICTION-USING PYTHON

    The Telco customer churn dataset contains information about a fictional telco company that provided home phone and Internet services to 7043 customers in California in Q3. It indicates which customers have left, stayed, or signed up for their service. Multiple important demographics are included for each customer. The objective of this project is to predict user behavior to retain customers. I extracted and fixed data errors using appropriate solutions to avoid skewing the entire dataset. I performed exploratory analysis (EDA) using Python to understand the reasons and causes of the churn rate. I used visualizations to understand the correlations among the variables against the target (Churn). I concluded this task by providing my observations and identified areas that needed further probing in order to reduce churn. Furthermore, I employed different models for better accuracy in predicting user churn behavior. In this project, Logistic Regression and XGBoost gave better accuracy of 80%.

    Company-x customer Care Record

    This is my Power BI project, aimed at achieving the following objectives for an organization offering products using loans: 1) To determine the peak times when most customers called the customer service center to resolve issues or other inquiries. 2) To analyze the number of customers the company interacts with on a daily basis and the frequency of customer calls to the sales, support, or success representative teams. Additionally, to identify the most common topics discussed during these calls. 3) Utilizing SQL to ascertain the number of loans per customer and identify customers with payments exceeding a certain threshold. I extracted the dataset from Excel, performed data cleaning, and conducted time conversions before loading it onto Power BI for analysis. Through the Power BI dashboard, I was able to observe that customer calls peaked around noon and gradually decreased after 6 pm. Furthermore, I utilized Excel to create a visualization depicting the days of the week and the frequency of customer calls from Sunday to Saturday.
